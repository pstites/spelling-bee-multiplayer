About this game
Our story

Before my father died, we had a daily ritual that involved playing the New York Times Spelling Bee game together every morning. I have many keepsakes of my father and our relationship, some big and some small, but one of my fondest keepsakes is actually quite mundane - screenshots of the many text messages we would send to each other while playing Spelling Bee. We would text triumphantly when one of us found a pangram, we would make up words and laugh about them - CATFART, for example. To this day, my phone will autocomplete the text "Pangram hint?" because he and I sent that one back and forth to each other so many times. Our text exchanges spanned hours at a time - I would leave for work and come back to it at the end of the day and we would pick up where we left off. Sometimes it's the casual and everyday interactions that end up meaning the most.

After he died, I found myself thinking about a version of the game that would honor that spirit of fun and easy collaboration, so my idea for this game started with what's called "collaborative mode" - in that mode you can play all day and work together to try to get to Genius or even find all the words in Queen Bee. Sometimes you might feel like a quick game and want to keep score, which is why I developed competitive mode with a 5-minute timer to keep the game quick and fun.

I hope you enjoy playing with your friends and family and making memories together!

How to play
🤝
Collaborative
Work together toward a shared score with no time limit. Either player can end the game whenever you're ready. Reaching Queen Bee (every word found) ends the game naturally.
⚔️
Competitive
Race to find words before your opponent. You have 5 minutes — the first player to spot a word claims the points. Highest score when time runs out wins.
